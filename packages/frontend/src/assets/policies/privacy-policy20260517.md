# KiwiClient Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** 17 May 2026

## 1. Who we are

KiwiClient is a non-commercial personal project operated by Luca Tremblay,
acting as sole trader and sole data controller for the purposes of the UK
General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.
We are not a company, do not generate revenue, and process personal data solely
to operate the KiwiClient waitlist.

You may contact the data controller by email at
[admin@kiwiclient.net](mailto:admin@kiwiclient.net). Where a postal address is
required for the exercise of your statutory rights, please request one by email
and we shall provide it.

## 2. What we collect

When you join the KiwiClient waitlist we collect only the following personal
data:

- the email address you provide to us; and
- the date and time on which you submitted that email address.

We do not collect names, IP addresses for analytics purposes, location data,
payment details, device identifiers, marketing preferences, browsing history,
or any other personal data. The landing page does not use cookies, analytics,
or tracking technologies.

## 3. Lawful basis

We rely on your consent (UK GDPR Article 6(1)(a)) as the lawful basis for
processing your email address. By submitting the waitlist form you consent to
receive a single confirmation message and, in due course, a single announcement
message when KiwiClient enters beta testing. You may withdraw your consent at
any time using the methods set out in section 6.

## 4. Purpose of processing

Your email address is used exclusively to:

- send you a one-time confirmation that you have joined the waitlist; and
- send you a single notification when KiwiClient becomes available for beta
testing.

We do not use your data for marketing unrelated to KiwiClient, profiling,
automated decision-making, or any secondary purpose.

## 5. Sub-processors and international transfers

We use Resend (operated by Plus Five Five, Inc., a company incorporated in the
United States) to deliver the confirmation and beta-launch emails described
above. Your email address is transmitted to Resend for the sole purpose of
delivering those messages.

Our processing relationship with Resend is governed by a Data Processing
Agreement which satisfies the requirements of UK GDPR Article 28 and which
incorporates the UK Addendum to the European Commission's Standard Contractual
Clauses for the purposes of UK GDPR Article 46. Resend is additionally
certified under the UK Extension to the EU-U.S. Data Privacy Framework and
holds SOC 2 Type II certification.

Resend is the only third party with which your personal data is shared. The
waitlist database is stored on a server located in the United Kingdom.

## 6. Retention

We retain your email address until the earlier of:

- your request for deletion, by clicking the unsubscribe link in any email we
send you, or by writing to [admin@kiwiclient.net](mailto:admin@kiwiclient.net);
or
- the dispatch of the beta-launch announcement email, after which the entire
waitlist database is permanently deleted.

## 7. Your rights

Under UK GDPR you have the right to: access the personal data we hold about you
(Article 15); have inaccurate data rectified (Article 16); have your data
erased (Article 17); restrict our processing of your data (Article 18); receive
your data in a portable format (Article 20); object to processing (Article 21);
and withdraw your consent at any time (Article 7(3)) without affecting the
lawfulness of processing carried out before withdrawal.

To exercise any of these rights, click the unsubscribe link in any email you
have received from us, or write to
[admin@kiwiclient.net](mailto:admin@kiwiclient.net). We shall respond within
one calendar month of receiving your request, in accordance with UK GDPR
Article 12(3).

## 8. Source code transparency

KiwiClient is free and open-source software, released under the GNU Affero
General Public License version 3.0. You may inspect the source code at any time
to verify exactly how your personal data is collected, stored, transmitted, and
deleted. The repository is publicly available; for the current canonical
location, please contact [admin@kiwiclient.net](mailto:admin@kiwiclient.net).

## 9. Children

KiwiClient is not directed at, and we do not knowingly collect personal data
from, persons under the age of 13. If you believe a child under 13 has
submitted personal data to us, please contact
[admin@kiwiclient.net](mailto:admin@kiwiclient.net) and we shall delete the
record promptly.

## 10. Security

The waitlist database is stored on a server located in the United Kingdom,
protected by access controls. All communications between your browser and our
service, and between our service and Resend, are encrypted in transit using
HTTPS (TLS). Resend holds SOC 2 Type II certification and is independently
penetration-tested at least annually. In the event of a personal data breach
affecting your information, we shall notify the Information Commissioner's
Office within 72 hours where required by UK GDPR Article 33, and shall notify
you without undue delay where the breach is likely to result in a high risk to
your rights and freedoms. Notwithstanding these measures, no transmission or
storage of data over the internet can be guaranteed to be entirely secure.

## 11. Beta access and email provider compatibility

When KiwiClient enters beta testing, the way your email address is processed will depend on which mail provider you use:

- **Self-hosted, private, or third-party IMAP/SMTP mail servers** — you connect KiwiClient to your own mail server using the credentials you already control. We never transmit your email address to any third party in order to enable this connection.

- **Gmail accounts** — Google requires us to register an OAuth application in order for KiwiClient to access Gmail on your behalf. Whilst KiwiClient remains a non-commercial personal project, that OAuth application will remain in Google's "Testing" mode, which permits a maximum of 100 individually approved test users at any one time. If you sign up to the waitlist using a Gmail address, and at the point the beta opens we have not yet reached this cap, we shall add your Gmail address to Google's Test Users list so that you can sign in. This addition is made within the Google Cloud Console interface; the act of adding your address to the Test Users list transmits that address to Google as the operator of the OAuth platform. If we have reached the 100-user cap, we shall notify you and recommend either a self-hosted mail server or running your own self-hosted instance of KiwiClient (which is permitted by the AGPL licence and for which you would register your own Google OAuth application). We have no commercial relationship with Google in respect of this processing; Google's own privacy practices are governed by Google's privacy policy.

- **Other major providers (Microsoft Outlook, Yahoo, etc.)** — support is planned for future releases. Where a provider requires us to register an OAuth application, the equivalent disclosure will be added to this policy before that integration goes live.

## 12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time to reflect changes to our
processing or to legal requirements. The effective date at the top of this
document indicates when the current version took effect. Material changes will
be communicated to subscribers by email where reasonably practicable.

## 13. Complaints

If you are dissatisfied with how we have handled your personal data, please
contact us at [admin@kiwiclient.net](mailto:admin@kiwiclient.net) in the first
instance so that we may address your concerns directly.

You also have a statutory right to lodge a complaint with the UK supervisory
authority for data protection. Further information about this right, and how it
may be exercised, is available at [ico.org.uk](https://ico.org.uk).
